# HealthTrends

**Turn a stack of lab PDFs into a story you can actually read.**

Most people collect blood test results for years and never see them as anything
but a table of numbers on a page. HealthTrends changes that. Log the results from
each test, and it shows you every biomarker as a clear line over time — in range,
borderline, or out — so you can finally see where things are heading instead of
decoding a printout.

It's calm, private, and built for real life: reviewing your history at home,
before a doctor's visit, or keeping an eye on the whole family.

## What you can do with it

- **See the trend, not the table.** Each biomarker becomes a clean line over
  months and years. Green, amber, red tells you in-range, borderline, or out at a
  glance.
- **Understand what's yours.** Ranges default to sensible values, but you can use
  your own lab's printed range — because that's the one your doctor is reading.
- **Browse your body.** An interactive 3D body map pins each result to the organ
  it relates to, so a thyroid or liver marker sits where you'd expect it.
- **Keep the story in context.** A timeline lines your test sessions up against
  your own life events, so a change has a "why" next to it.
- **Track the whole family.** Keep separate profiles for yourself and the people
  you look after, all from one account.
- **Walk into appointments prepared.** Generate a clean, doctor-ready summary of
  your recent results to bring to a visit.

## What it covers

70+ common biomarkers out of the box — cholesterol and glucose, thyroid, blood
count, liver and kidney, vitamins, iron, and hormones — with the everyday ones you
recognize surfaced first.

## What we believe

- **Your data is yours.** You can export everything (CSV or JSON) or delete your
  account, anytime, for free. No lock-in.
- **Clarity over alarm.** HealthTrends shows you the numbers and the trend. It
  isn't a diagnosis and it doesn't replace your doctor — it helps you have a
  better conversation with them.
- **Calm by design.** No hype, no clutter. Just your own health, made legible.
